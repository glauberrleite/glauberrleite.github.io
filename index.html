Heterogeneous workplace

Date: February 28, 2016
Keywords: IT, linux, experience

First month in my new work. Beginning to learn how things are done and how the technological structure is making the University alive. So, I built a question in my thoughts: "How can I make something new in a well-established place?". Then I noticed that the establishment relies intensely of Microsoft Windows softwares and technologies (except the development sector and some server's virtual machines which uses the rock-solid Debian). I feel more confortable working with free and open source software (for reasons that I may explain in other posts) and saw it as an opportunity to show and bring possibilities for actual and future open source adepts to enjoy the services that have been deprived of them because of problems in interoperability. Projects

RAMOS Ink

Converts InkML files received from a Livescribe Smartpen's penlet to image and/or video. RAMOS Ink was build from a research in the Center of Excellence for Social Technologies (NEES). The application is part of a bigger software called RAMOS, which have a more complete set of features to aid researchers in Brazil to study writing processes in real-time.
